04 — FAQ

Common questions

How does jurisdiction routing work?

When you submit a workload, you specify a jurisdiction policy (e.g., EU-West, Switzerland). The platform routes your job exclusively to verified nodes within that jurisdiction. Your data never leaves the specified region during processing.

What are verification levels?

Compute providers go through three verification tiers. Tier 1: identity + hardware attestation for standard workloads. Tier 2: facility audit + compliance training for GDPR workloads. Tier 3: dedicated infrastructure + SLA for enterprise and regulated workloads.

Is Archipelag.io GDPR compliant?

Yes. The platform is designed for GDPR compliance from the ground up. Jurisdiction routing ensures data residency, verified nodes provide processing guarantees, and complete audit trails document every execution for regulatory reporting.

Are you SOC 2 certified?

SOC 2 certification is on our Q4 2026 roadmap. Enterprise tier customers will have access to our compliance documentation and can request audit reports as we progress through certification.

How does this help with the EU AI Act?

Archipelag.io helps teams meet AI Act requirements through verified execution, audit trails, and transparency reporting. Our compliance toolkit (Q4 2026) will include AI Act-specific templates and documentation for regulatory submissions.

Do credits expire?

No. Credits never expire. Use them whenever you need them.

Do I need to verify my identity?

Identity verification (government ID + selfie) is required before submitting workloads or registering as a host. You can browse the marketplace and explore the platform without verification. We use Stripe Identity for secure verification and only store your verified name and country.
